About This Class

The question at hand: How might we address the lack of practical design education at Cornell? The problem we were facing at Cornell was, being a resesarch institute, the university focused more on acaedmia over industry application.

As such, a few of us created a course on digital product design that focused on solving a real life problem in the form of a case study. The course was 10 weeks long and university credited. The lectures focused on product-thinking, design process, and learning relevant design tools.

By the end, many students ended up pursuing design as a career at companies such as Facebook, Grubhub, Jet.com, IBM Maelstrom, and Amazon.

People Involved

Started in 2015, this course was created by fellow Cornell students Andrew Aquino and Nicole Calace who noticed a growing interest in the field from students outside of the traditional STEM-encompassing majors.

From there after Nicole graduated in 2016, I worked alongside Andrew Aquino, Haewon Hwang, and Sahil Khoja to help design, teach, and facilitate the curriculum for the class. Together, we worked on designing slides, assignments, and holding workshops on design tools for students.

Assignments and Grading

Assignments centered around creating an in-depth case showcasing each step of the design process from ideation to prototyping/testing. Assignments were written in Dropbox Paper where the the four of us (Andrew, Haewon, Me, and Sahil) were able to provide detailed annotations for feedback.

Communication and Mentoring

Communication between was facilitated through a Facebook group that included students, students who were auditing (or wanted to sit in on a specific topic/lecture), and people even outside of Cornell that heard about the class and wanted to partake in assignments.

In addition, each of us held weekly office hours to go over assignments, discuss our experiences, and offer whatever insight we could on design.
