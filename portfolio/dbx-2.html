Creating a centralized place for shared content

Dropbox | Product Designer | March '18 - Present

Project Overview

Working spec here

Goal

By creating a centralized place for shared content in Dropbox, we can increase activity and access to shared content positioning us as a place for WIP content versus finalized content.

Objectives

  • Achieve a baseline for retrieving shared content
  • Increase access and actions taken on shared content

People problems and goals

What are the user problems we want to address?

Today, content either lives in a user’s folder hierarchy or has to be tracked down through some combination of notifications, email, and dropbox.com/share. Collaborators often have different needs around content organization, and retrieving content is difficult even when it’s well structured.

What's the value add for the user and business?

Baseline goals.

  • As a receiver, I want to find content shared with me without needing to navigate a folder tree.
  • As a sender and receiver, I want clear metadata and easy search and filtering available at the point of retrieval so I can find content based on my memory of how I received it

User goals.

  • As a collaborator, I want to better understand what exactly needs to get done on a shared piece of content
  • As a collaborator, I want clear actions available at the point of retrieval so I can quickly move a project along

Layout explorations

With that I created a set of design explorations with the goal of giving the best method of selecting documents (either in the form of batch invites, or immediate CTAs)

1. Sidebar View

  • Best way to see selected items and selection list in same view and allows for later possibility to add personal message with invite (compacted flow vs. additional modal). However, possible tough engineering work (esp with only 2 engineers for the following sprint).

2. List Selection

  • Larger hit area and readability of document list. However, UI made it not immediately clear what selections are made (could be confusing especially given categorization of docs - Recently viewed, Created by me, Shared with me…).Also required additional modal to insert personal message

3. Actionable List

  • Quickly actionable and requires less friction for inviting to docs, but had the potential to lead to less documents invited than other explorations. This exploration could also lead to unintentional sharing of documents because there’s less friction involved with the invite action

4. Thumbnail View

  • Immediate preview of documents and able tp provide social context to the documents to share. Selecting documents is not as clearly defined and also less real estate to show documents

Results

What we predicted

  • We hypothesized that prompting existing team members to invite new users to relevant paper docs would lead to higher activation.
  • We predicted that if we make it easier for new users in existing teams to find content of value, we will increase team user activation.

What happened

  • Experiment proved overall successful. Our main success metric of team user activation had a relative lift of 15% — and retention had a relative lift of 20% — This experience was ultimately shipped.
